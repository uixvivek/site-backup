Building a Scalable Design System

September 28, 2023

As teams grow and products expand, maintaining visual consistency becomes increasingly difficult. We faced this challenge head-on when our enterprise application began accumulating hundreds of slightly varied buttons, disparate color hex codes, and inconsistent padding tokens.

The solution wasn't just to "design better," but to architect a system. A scalable design system acts as the single source of truth for both designers and developers. But building one is an exercise in restraint as much as creation.

Core Principles We Adopted

  • Start with Tokens, Not Components: Definining our underlying typography scales, color palettes, and spacing rules gave us a flexible foundation before touching a single button.
  • Embrace Constraint: Restricting choices (e.g., only 4 allowed margin sizes) dramatically reduces cognitive load and visual inconsistencies across screens.
  • Documentation is the Product: A design system is only as good as its documentation. We invested heavily in documenting the "why" and "when" alongside the "what" of every component.

The transition wasn't completely smooth. Adopting a strict system requires a cultural shift in how product teams collaborate. But the long-term velocity gains and the resulting cohesive user experience made the effort undeniably worthwhile.
